## 1. PNRR Mission 4 Component 1 — STEM Digital Budget 2026

## 2. MePA (Mercato Elettronico PA) — Listing Process + Price Floors September 2026

### 2.1 MePA fundamentals

- **MePA** = Mercato Elettronico della Pubblica Amministrazione
- **Operator**: Consip (subsidiary Ministry of Economy and Finance)
- **Platform URL**: www.acquistinretepa.it
- **Status**: most widespread e-procurement platform in Italy, **mandatory** for many public administrations including schools and universities
- **Categories covered**: ICT goods and services, software licenses, hardware, training, consulting

### 2.2 Listing process (high-level)

1. **Vendor registration**: legal entity + VAT (P.IVA) + DURC (regularity certificate) + camera di commercio
2. **Self-categorization**: select MePA bandi (categories) — for ELAB: "Beni e servizi per l'informatica e la connettività" + "Servizi di Formazione"
3. **Catalog publication**: upload products with pricing + technical specs
4. **Quality verification**: AGID + Consip review (~30-60 days)
5. **Maintenance**: keep catalog current, respond to RFQ (richieste di offerta) within mandated window

### 2.3 Price floors / market dynamics

- **No formal floor** — sellers set prices; market discipline via competing offers
- **AGID innovative procurement** facility: for novel solutions (AI tutors, simulators), a separate "innovation procurement" pathway exists with eligibility criteria
- **Best practice**: list at -10% to -15% vs commercial price for MePA visibility, account for 22% IVA (VAT)

### 2.4 Timeline for ELAB

- **Today (28 April 2026)**: Davide Fagherazzi has MePA expertise — leverage
- **Target listing date**: **30 May 2026** — captures PNRR final-quarter purchasing window
- **Steps**:
  - Week 1 (1-7 May): documentation prep, P.IVA confirm, DURC pull
  - Week 2 (8-14 May): MePA registration, category self-cat
  - Week 3 (15-21 May): catalog upload (3 SKUs: ELAB Starter Kit Volume 1, Vol 2 add-on, Vol 3 advanced)
  - Week 4 (22-28 May): AGID/Consip verification responses
  - **Buffer**: AGID review can take 30-60 days — start NOW to be safe

### 2.5 Certified cloud services (AGID Cloud Marketplace)

- AGID coordinates "Three-Year Plan for Information Technology in Public Administration 2024-2026"
- All certified cloud services listed on AGID's cloud marketplace
- **Implication for ELAB**: if Supabase + Vercel are NOT on AGID list, school CIOs may flag during procurement → mitigation: deploy Italian-hosted relay (Aruba, OVH IT) or document equivalence

## 3. Italian Regional STEM Coordinators — USR + RAV

### 3.1 USR (Uffici Scolastici Regionali)

- 18 regional education offices (one per region + 2 autonomous provinces)
- Each USR has STEM-coordination function: liaison schools ↔ MIM (Ministry)
- **Key contacts to develop**: Toscana, Piemonte (Strambino home turf), Lombardia (largest student count), Veneto (innovation hub), Emilia-Romagna (manufacturing + maker culture), Lazio (ministerial proximity)

### 3.2 RAV (Rapporto di Autovalutazione)

- Annual self-assessment report each school produces
- Includes "Aree dei processi" + "Esiti" — STEM/digital outcomes captured
- **Implication for ELAB**: RAV outcomes can include "adoption of innovative STEM platforms" → favorable benchmark if ELAB used → schools have INCENTIVE to demonstrate ELAB usage in RAV

### 3.3 Animatore Digitale per scuola

- 1 designated teacher per school institution (scuole pre-existing structure since 2015 PNSD)
- ELAB primary persona: Animatore Digitale (often vice-principal or mid-career ICT teacher)
- These individuals control €2,000 PNRR Animatori Digitali voucher per school
- **Direct sales channel**: Animatore Digitale ↔ ELAB free trial → conversion to paid kit

### 3.4 Existing networks

- **Equipe Formative Territoriali (EFT)**: regional STEM training cells, organize teacher training
- **Movimento Avanguardie Educative** (INDIRE): innovation school network ~1,500 schools, peer learning
- **Reggio Children network**: K-pre-school maker culture, distinct from K-12 STEM
- **Engagement strategy**: Giovanni Fagherazzi (ex Arduino Global Sales Director) likely has EFT + Avanguardie contacts

## 4. Competitor Mapping

### 4.1 Italian competitors

#### MyEdu (Genesis srl)
- Founded ~2014, partner Ministry of Education since 2018
- **Reach**: 3,900 schools, 450,000 licenses released
- **Coverage**: all subjects compulsory schooling (not STEM-only)
- **Pricing**: not publicly disclosed; private school MyEdu Family plan + institutional MyEdu Class
- **Strength**: brand recognition, ministerial partnership, long catalog
- **Weakness**: NO Arduino-physical-hardware integration, NO AI tutor, mostly content delivery
- **ELAB diff**: ELAB owns hardware-software integration (Arduino + simulator) + AI tutor

#### Deascuola (DeAgostini Scuola, Mondadori Group)
- Major textbook publisher pivoting to digital
- Platform: HUB Scuola (joint Mondadori Education + Rizzoli Education + Deascuola)
- **Strength**: textbook ecosystem lock-in, regional sales force
- **Weakness**: textbook-centric, weak interactive STEM, no maker/Arduino DNA
- **ELAB diff**: live circuits + AI tutor vs static digital textbook

#### bSmart
- Educational platform connecting publishers' digital books
- Partner of major publishers (Mondadori, Pearson, Zanichelli)
- **Strength**: publisher integration, distribution
- **Weakness**: same as Deascuola — content delivery only

#### SmartAula
- Less data publicly available — research returned no detailed information
- Possibly small regional player, not a primary threat

#### Wonder Robotics (Italy?)
- Research returned reference to Wonder Workshop (US), unclear Italian operation
- ELAB to monitor for emerging local players

### 4.2 International competitors

#### Tinkercad (Autodesk)
- **Pricing**: free
- **Strength**: drag-and-drop circuit editor, 100+ components, browser-based no install
- **Use case**: K-12 first-time Arduino learners
- **Weakness**: simulation-only, no real hardware bridge, no Italian curriculum, no AI tutor, no class-management
- **ELAB diff**: physical Arduino Nano + Italian curriculum + Lavagna + AI tutor

#### Wokwi
- **Pricing**: freemium (free tier + paid plans for advanced features)
- **Strength**: Arduino + ESP32 + Pi Pico + STM32 simulation, Wi-Fi/MQTT IoT simulation
- **Use case**: advanced makers, university-track high schoolers
- **Weakness**: complex UX for K-8, English only, no curriculum, no class management
- **ELAB diff**: K-8 friendly UX + Italian + curriculum + integration with physical kit

#### Adafruit IO
- **Pricing**: freemium ($10/mo Plus tier, free starter)
- **Strength**: IoT cloud + circuit playgrounds, vibrant community
- **Use case**: maker hobby, university
- **Weakness**: US-centric, English, no Italian curriculum, no class structure
- **ELAB diff**: Italian K-12 native

#### micro:bit (BBC + Foundation)
- **Pricing**: hardware ~£15, software free
- **Strength**: massive UK adoption, open-source, simple block coding
- **Use case**: K-6 entry programming
- **Weakness**: limited compared to Arduino's analog + sensor depth, micro:bit ecosystem locked
- **ELAB diff**: Arduino is broader-capable + Italian + AI tutor

### 4.3 Hardware competitors

#### Arduino Education (parent company arduino.cc)
- **Products**: Arduino Cloud for Schools, Arduino Science Journal (free), Science Kit, Education starter packs
- **Strength**: brand authority, ecosystem, Giovanni Fagherazzi's former employer (KEY ELAB ASSET)
- **Pricing**: not publicly disclosed for Cloud school plan; Science Journal is free; kits priced €100-300+
- **Weakness**: no AI tutor, no Italian-specific curriculum integration, English-first
- **ELAB diff**: Omaric (Italian filiera Arduino) + Giovanni's relationship + AI tutor + Italian K-12 curriculum mapped to MIM standards

#### Raspberry Pi Foundation
- **Strength**: massive global K-12 reach via Code Club + CoderDojo
- **Weakness**: Pi is a computer (Linux), not a microcontroller — less direct fit for circuit/STEM K-8 vs Arduino Nano
- **ELAB diff**: Arduino Nano R4 simpler for absolute beginners, Italian-localized

## 8. ICT Teacher Persona Analysis

### 8.1 Persona archetypes

#### A. "Maria, 52, Animatore Digitale, Liceo Scientifico Torino"
- 25 years teaching, last 5 years digital animation responsibility
- Tech adoption: medium (uses Google Classroom, struggles with new tools)

- Decision authority: medium (recommends to dirigente scolastico)

- Value props: "real Arduino hardware + simulator + AI debugging help"

1. **PNRR final-quarter is THE window**: April-June 2026 = peak school purchasing → MUST be MePA-listed by 30 May 2026
2. **Bundle is the differentiator**: ELAB hardware + software + AI + volumi + videocorsi = single-SKU offer; competitors fragmented (MyEdu = software only, Arduino = hardware only)
